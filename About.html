About Me

A recent graduate of the Ann and H.J. Smead Aerospace department at the University of Colorado Boulder. I have a passion for aerospace electronics, propulsions, and hardware. Currently working to design stronger and more reliable solutions for a global community, to better our lives both on planet Earth and throughout the solar system. In my spare time, I enjoy rock climbing, mountain biking, and backcountry skiing throughout the Colorado Rockies and American west.

Over the past four years of college I have made a countless amount of significant relationships with classmates, co-workers, and fellow club members. I held a co-president position within the Farm Club at CU, working to maintain a garden on CU's East Campus, growing food for students and faculty. I also held a board member position in the Alpine Club at CU, leading both climbing trips for students across Colorado, Wyoming, and Utah, as well as organizing community events within Boulder such as trash clean ups and local trail maintenance projects. Previous to college, I gained valuable leadership experience in my local scout troop, as a Senior Patrol Leader, while working my way to earn the rank of Eagle Scout. In addition to my leadership experiences, I enjoy time backcountry skiing, cycling, fly fishing, and spending time with my great pyrenees rescue.
